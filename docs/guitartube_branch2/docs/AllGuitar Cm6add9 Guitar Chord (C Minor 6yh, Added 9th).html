Cm6add9 guitar chord (C Minor 6yh, Added 9th)

Symbols: m6add9, m6/9, min6/9, m(add6,9), -6/9

Steps: 1-b3-5-6-9

Notes: C-Eb-G-A-D

The C m6add9 chord combines the richness of the minor 6th with the added 9th. With notes C, Eb, G, A and D, it creates a warm and jazzy sound that works beautifully in smooth transitions and improvisations. Below, you'll find three variations how to play the "C m6add9" chord, each featuring finger placements and an audio sample.

Variation 1
  • Place your pinky on the B (2nd) string at the 3rd fret and press down
  • Press down on the G (3rd) string at the 2nd fret using your middle finger
  • Position your index finger on the D (4th) string at the 1st fret and press down
  • Use your ring finger to press the A (5th) string down on the 3rd fret
  • Strum from the 5th string to the 2nd strings

Variation 2
  • Barre the 8th fret with your pinky, extending over the 1st to the 2nd strings
  • Barre the 7th fret with your middle finger, extending over the 3rd to the 4th strings
  • Press down on the A (5th) string at the 6th fret using your index finger
  • Now, strum all the strings starting from the 5th string

Variation 3
  • With your pinky, press down on the E (1st) string at the 10th fret
  • Press the B (2nd) string on the 10th fret with your ring finger
  • Use your index finger to press the G (3rd) string down on the 8th fret
  • Place your middle finger on the D (4th) string at the 10th fret and press down
  • Now, strum all the strings starting from the 4th string
